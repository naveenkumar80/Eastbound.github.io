Enchanting Nepal: Mountains, Culture,

and the Tapestry of Hindu-Buddhist Traditions

The earthquake that struck Nepal in 2015 left a deep impact on the history of this Himalayan kingdom, altering its course in ways unimaginable. However, through resilience and determination, much of the debris from Nepal's cherished historical sites, including Kathmandu's Basantapur Durbar Square and Patan Darbar Square, has been painstakingly cleared, and these magnificent sites are now once again open to the public. The road to recovery has not been easy, but with the unwavering support of tourists, Nepal is gradually reclaiming its former glory.

Himalayas

The Himalayas are the center of many fairytales, legends, stories and folklore. These beautiful majestic mountain ranges are filled with flora and fauna, small villages, glaciers and so much bird life that you can spend days exploring, walking, trekking or doing any adventure activity that strikes your fancy.

Adventure

Known as the hub for adventure sports, avid adrenaline junkies are drawn to Nepal for amazing hiking trails, trekking possibilities, mountain biking paths, bungee jumping, river rappelling or rafting, canyoning and the joy of doing all this amidst the best mountain landscapes you will ever find.

Wildlife

South of Nepal lie its dense national parks where you can spot exotic birds, animal species, diverse flora and fauna and do all this on the back of a gently swaying elephant or a grand jeep safari.
